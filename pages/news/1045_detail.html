What is POLYALUMINIUM CHLORIDE and what is its purpose?

POLYALUMINIUM CHLORIDE， abbreviated as PAC, which is an inorganic polymer compound flocculant with excellent flocculation performance. The following is detailed information about polyaluminum chloride:

Basic information:

CAS NO：1327-41-9; 101707-17-9; 11097-68-0; 114442-10-3

Molecular formula: [Al2 (OH) nCl6-n · xH2O] m (m ≤ 10, n=1-5)

Molecular weight: 174.45

Physical properties: colorless or yellow resin like solid, its solution is a colorless or yellow brown transparent liquid, sometimes appearing as a gray black mucus due to impurities.

Application:

Polyaluminum chloride is a commonly used water treatment chemical, widely used in water treatment, sewage treatment, advanced sewage treatment, and other liquid treatment applications.

It is mainly used for flocculation and solid-liquid separation processes, where aluminum ions and chloride ions react chemically with impurity particles, colloids, and suspended solids in water to form flocculents, which then aggregate and precipitate to remove impurities from the water.

Performance characteristics:

Efficient flocculation performance: Polyaluminum chloride can quickly form large and compact flocs, improving solid-liquid separation efficiency.

Good stability: suitable for different water quality and temperature conditions.

Easy to operate: Easy to use, without the need for complex equipment and operation processes.

Product standards:

The quality of drinking water products meets the national GB15892-2009 standard.

The technical indicators include aluminum oxide (AL2O3) content, alkalinity, water insoluble content, pH value, etc. The specific values vary depending on the product level and application field.

Security information:

Polyaluminum chloride is safe under normal usage conditions, but direct contact with skin, eyes, etc. should be avoided. If in contact, rinse immediately with water and seek medical attention.

Main use:

POLYALUMINIUM CHLORIDE is an inorganic polymer coagulant, mainly used in the fields of water treatment and wastewater treatment. The following are the main uses of polyaluminum chloride:

Drinking water treatment: Polyaluminum chloride can effectively remove suspended solids, colloids, pigments, organic matter, and heavy metal ions from water, improve water quality, and make it meet drinking water standards.

Industrial wastewater treatment: Polyaluminum chloride is widely used in industrial wastewater treatment and can treat various types of wastewater, such as papermaking wastewater, printing and dyeing wastewater, electroplating wastewater, chemical wastewater, etc. It can remove pollutants from wastewater through adsorption, neutralization, flocculation and other actions, reduce COD (chemical oxygen demand), BOD (biological oxygen demand) and chromaticity in wastewater, and meet discharge standards or reuse requirements.

Municipal sewage treatment: Polyaluminum chloride is used as a coagulant in municipal sewage treatment, which can effectively remove suspended solids, fats, chromaticity, and organic matter in sewage, improving the treatment efficiency and water quality of sewage.

Swimming pool water treatment: Polyaluminum chloride can be used for the purification and treatment of swimming pool water quality, removing suspended solids, algae, and other organic substances in the water, and maintaining clear and hygienic water quality.

Paper industry: In the paper industry, polyaluminum chloride can be used as a paper sizing and sedimentation agent, retention and filtration aid, which can improve the dry and wet strength of paper, reduce shrinkage, improve printing performance, and improve paper quality.

Other fields: Polyaluminum chloride can also be used for the treatment of oilfield reinjection water, refinery wastewater, coal washing wastewater, and oily wastewater, as well as for the treatment of wastewater from casting, sand turning, reclaimed water reuse, pharmaceuticals, leather making, dyes, and other industries.

Overall, polyaluminum chloride has a wide range of applications in water and wastewater treatment, effectively removing pollutants from water, improving water quality, and protecting the environment.
