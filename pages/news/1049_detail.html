Basic information and industry applications of sodium nitrate and formic acid



Sodium Nitrate, NaNO3

Basic information:

Sodium nitrate is an inorganic salt that appears as a colorless transparent or white slightly yellowish diamond shaped crystal with hygroscopicity. It is easily soluble in water, liquid ammonia, and can dissolve in methanol and ethanol. The melting point of sodium nitrate is 306.8 ℃. When heated to 380 ℃, it will decompose to produce sodium nitrite and oxygen. Sodium nitrate has oxidizing properties and may cause combustion or explosion due to friction or impact with organic matter. It should be stored properly

Industry Usage:

  • Glass industry: used as a defoamer, decolorizer, clarifier, and oxidizing flux in the production of glass and glass products
  • Enamel industry: used as a flux, oxidant, and raw material for preparing enamel powder
  • Mechanical industry: used as a metal cleaning agent to prepare black metal blueing agents
  • Metallurgical industry: used as a heat treatment agent for steelmaking and aluminum alloys
  • Fertilizer industry: used as a fast fertilizer for acidic soils
  • Other: It can also be used as analytical reagents, spectroscopic reagents, oxidants, and for dye synthesis and the manufacture of explosives and fireworks


Formic Acid (HCOOH)

Basic information:

Formic acid is an organic acid, also known as formic acid. It is a colorless and pungent liquid, corrosive, and can be mixed with water, as well as ethanol, ether, glycerol, and other substances. Formic acid is flammable, and its vapor can form an explosive mixture with air. When exposed to open flames and high heat, it can cause combustion and explosion

Industry Usage:

  • Rubber industry: used as a coagulant for natural rubber
  • Leather industry: used for tanning, de liming, and dyeing of leather
  • Textile industry: used as dyeing aids, anti shrink agents, rust removers, etc
  • Chemical industry: used to produce various products such as formates, formamides, formates, acetic acid, oxalic acid, ethylene glycol, etc
  • Pesticide industry: used for producing fungicides, insecticides, etc
  • Other: It can also be used as an intermediate in medicine, dyes, spices, etc

Sodium nitrate and formic acid are widely used in their respective fields, but due to their certain hazards, safety operating procedures should be strictly followed when using them
